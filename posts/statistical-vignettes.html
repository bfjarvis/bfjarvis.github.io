A template for reproducible analysis vignettes

Draft post · Replace with publication date

Statistical vignettes are useful when a full paper is too heavy but a short claim still deserves a transparent empirical trail. Each vignette should show the research question, data, estimand or target quantity, model, sensitivity checks, and limits.

Question

State the substantive question in plain language. For segregation and stratification research, this usually means naming the population, spatial or institutional scale, outcome, and comparison being made.

Data and Measurement

Describe the data source, sample restrictions, time period, missingness decisions, and how core concepts become variables. Flag measurement choices that could change interpretation.

Model

Explain the model as a bridge between the question and the evidence. Give enough detail for a reader to understand what is being estimated without requiring them to reconstruct the analysis from code alone.

Interpretation

Translate the estimate into a substantive statement. Report uncertainty directly and make clear which comparisons are descriptive, associational, or intended as causal.

Sensitivity

Close with the checks that would most change your confidence: alternate specifications, scale choices, functional forms, sample restrictions, and robustness to influential cases or places.
